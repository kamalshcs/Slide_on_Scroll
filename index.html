Valley of Flowers

This breathtaking landscape of nature is a part of Nanda Devi and Valley of Flowers National Parks, located in North Chamoli, Uttrakhand. It is recognized as a ‘World Heritage Site’ by UNESCO. The Valley of Flowers offers a spectacular view of thriving flora and fauna every year for thousands of tourists from across the world. It is one of the favorite sites for nature lovers who love to bask in the magnificent glory of God’s creation. A perfect example of tranquility amongst the snow-covered mountains of Chamoli. This vista is a home to a variety of species of birds and flowers.

Tourists visit the Valley of flowers for the love of trekking. It is a 17 km, high-altitude trek between the terrific mountains. The journey encompasses dense forests, rivers, bridges, and glaciers, leaving the trekkers in awe of the lush scenery. Also, it is an ideal attraction for photographers. The valley allows you to capture some of the most extraordinary pictures of rare flowers and birds native to the region. Trekking and Wildlife photography are some of the must-have activities along with sightseeing. Camping is another favorite adventure amongst the people who go for a trip to the Valley of flowers.

This trip is an 11-hour drive from Dehradun to Joshimath, and another hour to Gobindghat. From Gobindghat, it is a 13 km trek along a steep, narrow, but well-defined mountain trail to base camp at Ghangaria. Then, from Ghangaria, another 3 to 4 km trek leads to the valley.

This Indian National Park opens for only four months that is, from June to October. You can enjoy the marvelous wonders of this valley during July and August. It is in full bloom during these months. Planning a trip to the Valley of flowers will be an incredible option for these vacations.
